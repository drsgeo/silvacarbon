2021 Activities

Forest Heights and Mangrove Mapping
September 1-3, 2021: Colombia

Dr. Lola Fatoyimbo and Marc Simmard worked with IDEAM to create height maps for wetlands areas in the pacific region. Results will be delivered at a training at the Google Earth Engine Summit.

CODED degradation training
August 3-4, 2021

Dr. Eric Bullock from the US Forest Service introduced the Continuous Degradation Detection Algorithm (CODED) and the results on the Oromia region. These two-hour sessions took place virtually on August 3 and August 4 using Microsoft Teams.

Mexico Biomass Consultation

USGS SilvaCarbon is testing the use of land cover global datasets in Mexico in collaboration with WRI. The results will assist CONAFOR in complementing country-based data with global defaults based on existing gaps of information. Mexico delegates will present a report during the COP-26.

Regional Central America GEE 101 training in collaboration with McGill University
June 16-17, 2021

The USGS SilvaCarbon in collaboration with McGill University, Boston University, and Google Earth Engine supported technical capacity for countries in Central America to produce robust activity data. With this training countries are learning and testing open-source algorithms to map land use change. In the Zoom-based training session, GEE was introduced and walked through exercises on how to use it.

Google Earth Engine 101 training
June 16-17, 2021: Solomon Islands

SilvaCarbon, in collaboration with McGill University and Google Earth Engine, supported technical capacity building for the Solomon Islands REDD+ Implementation Unit and other related agencies in the Solomon Islands and other Pacific countries. In 2019, The Solomon Islands submitted a National Forest Reference Level (FRL) to the UNFCCC, the first effort by the Solomon Islands to estimate carbon emissions from the land and forestry sector. SilvaCarbon is assisting them in tackling the improvements suggested by the UNFCCC and increasing the country's technical capacity.

Technical assistance on wetlands mapping
June 8, 2021: Colombia

IDEAM in Colombia is working closely with USGS and NASA to integrate wetlands into their National Forest Monitoring System. SilvaCarbon has supported NASA to transfer the capacity to IDEAM on generating height wetlands maps and cover the data gaps on the Pacific coast.

Technical assistance on using global datasets for AFOLU reporting
June 4, 2021: Peru

The Ministry of Environment and the Forest Service of Peru are working together with SilvaCarbon and the CEOS AFOLU team to test the OLU mangrove global dataset from ESA and the CCI Biomass Global Dataset from JAXA. These two global datasets provided complementary historical data to fill the gaps of information in the coast and the Andean Amazon region of Peru.

Technical Assistance - Crop Commodities Mapping
June 4, 2021: Paraguay

The University of Maryland is developing metrics to differentiate commodity crops in the Chaco region in Paraguay. This effort will assist INFONA in associating specific emission factors with remote sensing metrics for high-value crops and improving estimates' accuracy that allowed to move to tier 3 reporting.

Biomass Consultation
May 12, 2021: Paraguay

USGS SilvaCarbon is working with INFONA in Paraguay and the CEOS AFOLU group to test a methodology that uses space Lidar to augment National Forest Inventory Data and improve emission factors nationally. This initiative required a few virtual trainings and consultations, and results will be delivered at the COP–26.

Biomass Consultation Harmonization
May 3, 2021: Paraguay

USGS SilvaCarbon is testing the use of land cover global datasets in Paraguay in collaboration with WRI. The results will assist INFONA in complementing country-based data with global defaults based on existing gaps of information. Paraguay delegates will present a report during the COP-26.

Mapping consultation in Ethiopia
May and April 27, 2021: Ethiopia

SilvaCarbon is working with the Ethiopia – Oromia region on testing different methods to map deforestation and forest degradation that can be scaled to the National Level and integrated into the SEPAL platform to use for the Forest Reference Levels submission. SilvaCarbon is urrently assisting with testing methods and ata collection in collaboration with FAO.

Technical Assistance Ecuador Forest Reference Levels
Three workshops: May 3, May 6, and July 5, 2021

SilvaCarbon organized three consultations with the Ministry of Environment of Ecuador, Boston University, and USGS to review technical aspects of the deforestation area estimation results.

University of Maryland (UMD)
February 23, 2021: Peru

SilvaCarbon works with the University of Maryland to attend a request from the Ministry of Environment to map deforestation in the Amazon. This effort complemented the new mapping methodology that MINAM implemented for the second submission of their Forest Reference Levels.

University of Maryland (UMD)
February 17, 2021: Guatemala

The University of Maryland generated metrics to differentiate palm from forest using Landsat data. These new metrics were applied and use in Guatemala in the National Forest Monitoring System. They will be included in their first National Forest Reference Level. In Guatemala Palm is included under agriculture classification and separating them from Forest improves the accuracy of the deforestation estimates.

Sampling-Based Estimation of Area and Map Accuracy
January 8, 2021: Republic of Congo

SilvaCarbon, through the USGS in partnership with Boston University, developed an E-learning course to introduce the concept, rationale, and steps of sampling-based estimation. In January, the online course was delivered to national experts in RoC who are involved in the ongoing national land-use change assessment. The course was delivered through a workshop format to enable participants to easily access course materials and exchange directly with the course instructor, Professor Pontus Olofsson of Boston University.
